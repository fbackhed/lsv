The facility is located on the moon to protect seeds from future threats on planet earth. Japanese scientist was the first to find the underground lava tubes on the moon which may hold a suitable environment for a seed vault. The lava tubes can protect the seed vault agains radient from outerspace, falling astroids and extreme tempratures. The temprature is more stable in the tubes with a temprature between -30 and -40 degrees which can help the storage of the seeds.

Lunar Seed Vault is a part of the Svalbard Seed Vault company. The norwegian government funds the seed vault with operative help from Crop Trust. Nordgen is responsible for the management of the seed vault and seed operation. Statsbygg hired by the Norwegian government is responisble for maintenance of the facility, day-to-day surveillance and monotoring.
